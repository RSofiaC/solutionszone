Algae Globe

Far out on the horizon--but so interesting--is Algae. Another renewable resource that can be transformed into biofuel, fertilizer, and has the possibility of capturing carbon from the atmosphere. As a living organism it holds a symbiotic relationship with us which can be experienced through the interaction of this exhibit.

channeling waste


say something

Waste is not a noun as much as a framework. The bi-products of any activity can be reintegrated into the value chained or transformed into new products. This exhibits aims to show how the residues that we produce through every flush can be turned into fuel for transportation, power and fertilizers for Agriculture.






Solar Silk

A light installation that hovers above the section about Renewable Energies celebrating solar energy, one of the most prevalent and promising alternative energy resources. Solar Silk visualizes real time UV index data that indicates the amount of potential solar energy at Fort Mason in San Francisco where the piece is installed. The changing colors and patterns of the light reflect the availability of solar energy to be harvested.

Even though it delivers instant information, it is through long time observation that visitors can perceive the change of energy available and understand the scale to which solar energy can generate an impact in our lives.

regenerative agriculture


soil carbon

There are two major ways we can reduce carbon emissions: renewable energies is one. Carbon Capture is the other. The soil plinth shows how soil draws down carbon and stores it deep in the earth. The exhibit in the fruit crates shows through short videos how if we change (or revert back to traditional) farming methods we can regenerate the soil and not release carbon in the air.
